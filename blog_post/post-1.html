How to Make Authentic Vietnamese Pho at Home

By PhohamLiverpool | 29 Sep 2024

Pho is a traditional Vietnamese noodle soup that has become globally popular thanks to its rich flavor and nourishing ingredients. In this post, we‚Äôll walk you through the steps to make an authentic bowl of pho at home.

Start by choosing quality beef bones and simmering them for hours with spices like star anise, cinnamon, and cloves. Don't forget to char the ginger and onion for that signature depth of flavor!

Ingredients:

  • 2 kg beef bones (marrow or knuckle)
  • 500g beef brisket or chuck
  • 2 onions, halved
  • 1 large piece of ginger (about 100g), halved
  • 3 star anise, 1 cinnamon stick, 4 cloves, 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tbsp salt, 1 tbsp sugar, 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • Rice noodles (pho noodles)
  • Fresh herbs: Thai basil, cilantro, scallions
  • Bean sprouts, lime wedges, chili slices (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Blanch the bones: Boil beef bones in water for 5 minutes. Drain and rinse bones under cold water to remove impurities.
  2. Char aromatics: Grill onions and ginger over open flame or in oven until charred. This adds depth to the broth.
  3. Simmer broth: In a large pot, add bones, 4-5 liters of water, charred onion and ginger, spices (tied in cheesecloth), and brisket. Bring to boil, then simmer gently for at least 6 hours.
  4. Season: Add salt, sugar, and fish sauce to taste during the last hour of simmering.
  5. Prepare noodles: Cook rice noodles according to package, then rinse with cold water and drain.
  6. Slice beef: Remove brisket and slice thinly. For raw beef topping, use eye-round or sirloin, sliced paper-thin.
  7. Assemble: In each bowl, add noodles, sliced brisket, raw beef, and hot broth. Garnish with herbs, sprouts, lime, and chili.
